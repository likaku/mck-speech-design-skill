# Context Gathering Checklist

Complete reference for all information to collect during the briefing stage. Ask questions incrementally — do not dump the entire list at once. Prioritize based on what the user has already provided.

## 1. Speaker Profile (Persona)

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Full name & title | For self-introduction scripting | "What's your name and title as you'd like to introduce yourself?" |
| Organization & division | Establishes authority | "Which organization/team do you represent?" |
| Your role in this meeting | Determines voice authority level | "Are you the lead presenter, one of several, or a guest?" |
| Professional background (1-2 lines) | For credibility-building moments | "What's the one credential that would matter most to this audience?" |
| Presentation style preference | Shapes tone and pacing | "Do you prefer formal/polished, conversational, or data-heavy?" |
| Personal anecdotes available | For authenticity | "Any personal stories or experiences you'd like woven in?" |

## 2. Audience Profile

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Who is in the room | Tailors depth and vocabulary | "Who's attending — titles, seniority level, how many people?" |
| Audience's organization | Shapes framing and relevance | "Which company/org are they from? What do they do?" |
| Audience's knowledge level | Avoids over/under-explaining | "How familiar are they with your domain?" |
| What they care about | Drives content selection | "What are their top 3 priorities or pain points?" |
| Likely skepticisms | Pre-empts objections | "What doubts might they walk in with?" |
| Decision-making authority | Shapes call-to-action | "Can they make decisions, or do they report up?" |

## 3. Relationship Context

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Prior interactions | Determines rapport baseline | "Have you met this audience before? How many times?" |
| Relationship stage | Shapes openness/formality | "Is this a first meeting, ongoing partner, or renewal?" |
| Previous commitments/promises | Avoids contradictions | "Anything discussed before that we should reference or avoid?" |
| Existing agreements or projects | Provides proof points | "Any joint work already underway?" |
| Relationship dynamics | Adjusts assertiveness level | "Is this relationship peer-to-peer, or is one side 'pitching' to the other?" |

## 4. Event & Format

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Event type | Shapes structure and tone | "What kind of event? (keynote, workshop, roundtable, 1:1, pitch)" |
| Total speaking time | Determines depth and pacing | "How long is your slot?" |
| Q&A included | Affects content allocation | "Is there Q&A time? How long?" |
| Slide deck available | Determines script-to-slide alignment | "Do you have slides already, or should I create the structure?" |
| Number of slides / pages | Constrains per-slide timing | "How many slides?" |
| Venue / setting | Formal vs casual tone | "Where is this? (boardroom, conference stage, virtual)" |
| Other speakers | Positioning relative to others | "Who else is presenting? What are they covering?" |

## 5. Content & Objectives

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Core message (one sentence) | Anchors the entire speech | "If the audience remembers one thing, what should it be?" |
| Top 3 objectives | Structures the arc | "What are you trying to achieve? (inform, persuade, build trust, close deal)" |
| Must-include topics | Prevents omissions | "What absolutely must be covered?" |
| Must-avoid topics | Prevents landmines | "Anything off-limits or politically sensitive?" |
| Key data points | Provides proof | "What numbers/facts should be highlighted?" |
| Case studies / examples | Anchors abstract points | "Any specific stories, projects, or clients to reference?" |
| Call to action | Defines the close | "What do you want the audience to DO after this?" |
| Supporting materials | Provides source truth | "Do you have any reference docs, previous decks, or briefs?" |

## 6. Language & Delivery

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Primary language | Determines script language | "What language will you deliver in?" |
| Audience's language(s) | May require simplified phrasing | "Does the audience speak this language natively?" |
| Technical jargon comfort | Calibrates vocabulary | "Should I use industry terms freely, or keep it accessible?" |
| Cultural considerations | Avoids missteps | "Any cultural norms to observe? (formality, humor, directness)" |

## 7. Tone & Style Preferences

| Item | Why It Matters | Example Prompt |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Desired tone | Overall feel | "Formal, conversational, inspirational, data-driven, or a mix?" |
| Humor level | Risk calibration | "Is humor appropriate? If so, how much?" |
| Storytelling vs facts | Structural preference | "Do you prefer narrative flow or structured data presentation?" |
| Self-Q&A style | Engagement technique | "Would you like to use rhetorical questions to guide the audience?" |
| Level of self-promotion | Trust vs persuasion balance | "Should this feel like a sharing session or more like a pitch?" |
