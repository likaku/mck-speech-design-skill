## Opening & Framing — Slide 1

### Purpose
Establish shared context, name the problem, and present the core thesis.

### Talking Points
- Acknowledge the audience's deep domain expertise as a valuable asset
- Name the problem: knowledge scattered across systems, people, and silos
- Present core thesis: AI can turn "sleeping assets" into a "business engine"

### Script

Good afternoon, everyone.

Today I'd like to share with you a framework that we've seen work across dozens of organizations — and I believe it's directly applicable to your current challenge.

Let me start with a fact we can all agree on: your team has accumulated **15 years** of deep domain expertise. That's a massive asset. But here's the complication — most of that knowledge lives in scattered documents, in email threads, in people's heads. When someone leaves, that knowledge walks out the door.

So our core thesis today is simple: with the right AI-powered knowledge system, we can **turn that sleeping asset into a business engine**.

How? Two key value propositions that I'll walk you through.

### Transition → Next Section
> "Let me start with the first: how daily work can automatically generate knowledge assets."

## Value Proposition 1: Work = Knowledge Capture — Slide 2

### Purpose
Demonstrate the "automatic knowledge capture" capability with concrete proof points.

### Talking Points
- Traditional KM fails because it requires extra effort from employees
- New approach: knowledge captured as a byproduct of normal work
- Proof point: 7,000+ documents auto-processed daily
- Map this to the audience's specific use cases

### Script

The first value proposition is what we call "**work equals knowledge capture**."

Traditionally, knowledge management relies on people manually documenting what they know. We all know how that goes — it starts strong, then falls off a cliff within three months.

Our approach is fundamentally different. Instead of asking people to do extra work, the system captures knowledge as a byproduct of normal daily activities.

Let me give you a concrete example. After a client meeting, the CRM notes are automatically parsed by AI, key insights are extracted, and they're filed into the relevant knowledge base — categorized by client, industry, and topic. No manual effort required.

The numbers speak for themselves: in one deployment, we saw **7,000+ documents** auto-processed daily with zero manual intervention.

### Transition → Next Section
> "That's the capture side. But knowledge only has value when it's used — which brings me to the second point."

## Value Proposition 2: Knowledge → Business Results — Slide 3

### Purpose
Show how captured knowledge drives measurable business outcomes.

### Talking Points
- Knowledge capture alone isn't enough — it must drive action
- Auto-briefing before client meetings: full context delivered proactively
- Measurable impact: 40% faster onboarding, 15-20% higher conversion
- Knowledge as a force multiplier

### Script

The second value proposition: **turning accumulated knowledge into business results**.

Capturing knowledge isn't the goal — using it to drive outcomes is.

Imagine this: before a sales rep walks into a meeting, the system automatically retrieves everything relevant — past interactions with this client, industry benchmarks, competitive intelligence, winning playbooks from similar deals. The rep walks in armed with the full picture, not just their own memory.

We've seen this reduce new employee ramp-up time by **40%** and increase deal conversion rates by **15-20%**.

The key insight here is that knowledge becomes a **force multiplier**. One person's experience becomes everyone's advantage.
